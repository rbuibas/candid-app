# 01 — Product Brief

## Vision

A shared, spontaneous photo/video diary for a group during a single event. Everyone gets pinged at random moments to capture what's *actually* happening around them. The result is an unscripted, collective record of the trip — assembled automatically, from everyone's perspective, with no one playing photographer.

## The core bet

Take the random, time-boxed prompt mechanic of daily-capture apps and reframe it as **group-scoped and event-scoped**, with each person prompted at their *own* random time. Because the timing is independent per person, you don't get everyone pointing cameras at each other on cue — you get candid shots of the group mid-activity, from many angles, across the whole event.

The wedge is the **prompt mechanic itself**. The honest competitor is not another social app — it's "just make a group chat / shared album," where everyone is already installed at zero friction. The prompt-driven candidness has to be good enough to justify installing something new for a few days. If the captures feel staged or the loop feels like a chore, the product has no reason to exist.

## Target user

- **Primary:** small friend groups at a defined event — bachelor/ette parties, group trips, weddings, festivals, reunions.
- **Group size:** typically 5–20 people.
- **Duration:** a weekend to ~two weeks, with a defined start and end.
- **First real use:** a bachelor party in Kraków, distributed privately to a handful of friends (TestFlight + APK), not the public.

## Operating principles

1. **Authenticity over polish.** Live capture only, short windows, no retakes-culture, no filters. The slightly-blurry real moment beats the staged one. Friction that protects spontaneity is a feature.
2. **Low friction, high spontaneity.** A prompt arrives, the camera is already open, you have a few minutes, no captions required. Contributing should cost almost nothing.
3. **Anti-doomscroll.** No likes, no follower counts, no infinite feed pressure. You contribute and you look back — that's the entire surface.
4. **Ephemeral pressure, lasting memory.** The capture window is fleeting; the archive lasts for the life of the event group.
5. **The group is the boundary.** Everything is scoped to one group. Nothing is public. There is no global discovery and never will be.
6. **Private by default.** Invite-only, EU-resident data, minimal PII. The product handles images of people and their locations — treat that data with care even before it's a public, regulated concern.

## What success looks like

For the MVP (the Kraków party), success is qualitative and behavioral, not metrics-on-a-dashboard:

- **High capture rate** — most prompts answered before they expire. This is the single health signal; if it's low, nothing else matters.
- **Broad participation** — most members posting most days, not one or two power users carrying the feed.
- **The "remember when" moment** — the group actually goes back through the feed together after the event, and it captured things no one would have thought to photograph.
- **Word of mouth** — someone in the group wants to run it for *their* next event.

## What this is NOT

Holding the line here is what keeps the product coherent:

- **Not a social network.** No public profiles, no global feed, no discovery, no followers.
- **Not a feed to perform on.** No likes, comments, or reactions. Nothing to optimize for, nothing to farm.
- **Not a photo manager.** No gallery uploads, no albums-of-albums, no editing suite. Capture is live and in-the-moment, period.
- **Not always-on.** It's event-scoped and designed to go dormant between events. That's intentional, not a retention failure to fix with engagement hacks.
- **Not a surveillance tool.** Location is optional and exists to power a future map of shared memories, not to track people.
- **Not (yet) a public, monetized product.** v1 is a private MVP for one friend group. Monetization, moderation infrastructure, and the full compliance surface are deliberately deferred (see `05-future-features.md`).
